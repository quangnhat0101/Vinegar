- RECIPE OF THE MONTH -

For those who do not have time for going out to eat, in order for you to enjoy the taste of Vinegar Food Restaurantat home, we would like to introduce some easy-to-made but delicious recipes created by our talented chef.

Cheddar Dill Puffs

Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup water
  2. 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
  3. 1 cup all-purpose flour
  4. 4 to 5 large eggs
  5. 1 1/2 cups finely grated yellow Cheddar
  6. 2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh dill

How to make:

First, the dough is cooked on the stovetop, removed from the heat, and then cooled slightly. It’s important to cool the dough a little bit so that the eggs will incorporate smoothly into the dough and not become scrambled eggs. At this point, the dough will look like a big lump of dry clay. That’s OK.

Then the eggs get beaten in one at a time. When you first add in each egg, the dough will separate and look completely messed up like what you see in the top right photo above. Don’t worry – just keep stirring. As the egg gets incorporated, the dough will get smoother and will magically become more glossy.

Be sure to bake the puffs until they feel slightly crispy when tapped. If you take them out too soon, the puffs will collapse.

Bouillabaisse

Ingredients:

  1. 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  2. 1 fennel bulb, halved and thinly sliced, fronds reserved
  3. Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper
  4. 4 cups seafood stock
  5. One 28-ounce can crushed tomatoes
  6. Zest and juice of 1 orange
  7. Pinch saffron
  8. 1 bay leaf
  9. 2 pounds cod, chopped
  10. 2 pounds shrimp, cleaned and peeled

How to make:

In a large stockpot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the leeks and fennel and sauté until tender, about 4 minutes. Season with salt and pepper. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute. Add the seafood stock, tomatoes, orange zest and juice, saffron and bay leaf. Bring to a simmer and cook until the liquid has reduced slightly, 12 to 15 minutes.

Add the cod and simmer for 2 minutes. Add the shrimp and mussels and cover the pot. Simmer until the seafood is fully cooked (the fish will be firm, the shrimp will be pink, and the mussels will be open), about 4 minutes. Discard any unopened mussels. Stir in the parsley. Top with the reserved fennel fronds and serve with the bread.

Moules-Frites (Steamed Mussels and Fries)

Ingredients:

  1. 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  2. Zest and juice of 1 lemon
  3. 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  4. 3 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley
  5. 2 pounds mussels, debearded and cleaned well
  6. 2 celery stalks, thinly sliced
  7. 2 leeks, thinly sliced
  8. 1/2 cup seafood broth or water
  9. 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  10. Salt and freshly ground black pepper

How to make:

MAKE THE AIOLI: In a small bowl, whisk the mayonnaise with the lemon zest, lemon juice, pepper and parsley to combine.

MAKE THE MOULES-FRITES:
1.In a large skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the celery and leeks, and sauté until tender, 3 to 4 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté until fragrant, 1 minute more.
2. Stir the tomato paste into the pan and cook for 1 minute. Add the mussels and toss gently with the other ingredients in the pan. Add the white wine and broth, and bring the mixture to a simmer over medium heat. Season with salt and pepper and add the parsley.
3. Cover the pan and simmer until the majority of mussels have opened, 5 to 6 minutes. Discard any mussels that do not open.
4. Divide the mussels between two bowls and pour any remaining broth and vegetables over the mussels. Serve immediately with fries and aioli.
